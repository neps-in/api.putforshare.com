1. SENDING FREQUENCY AND TRIGGERS
---------------------------------
Email is sent only in response to a user action; we do not schedule, batch, or bulk-send.
  - Email verification: sent once, immediately, when a user clicks "Sign up" and submits the form.
  - Password reset: sent once, only when the user clicks "Forgot password" and submits their address.
  - Welcome onboarding: sent once, when the user clicks the verification link in (1) and the address transitions to verified.
There are no marketing, newsletter, digest, or re-engagement emails. Expected day-1 volume is well under 100 messages/day, scaling with organic signups.

2. RECIPIENT LIST MANAGEMENT
----------------------------
We do not maintain mailing lists and we do not import recipient data from any third party. Each "recipient" is a single user who has just performed an action on our platform using their own email address. Account creation on PutForShare requires the user to enter their own email and click a verification link — that is our sole consent mechanism. There is no way for one user to add another user to email delivery.

3. BOUNCE HANDLING
------------------
Bounces are processed by our Django backend (`apps.notifications`) via Anymail's tracking signal (`apps/notifications/signals.py`):
  - Every SES bounce notification is persisted in a `SesEvent` audit row with the full bounce subtype (Permanent / Transient / Undetermined) and diagnostic.
  - On a Permanent bounce, the recipient address is added to the `SuppressedEmail` table with reason = HARD_BOUNCE. This table is queried before EVERY outgoing send; suppressed addresses are skipped at the application layer, before SES is invoked.
  - Transient (soft) bounces are retried up to 5 times via Celery with exponential backoff, then marked as `gave_up` (not retried further).
  - The account-level SES suppression list is also enabled for BOUNCE reasons as a defence in depth.

4. COMPLAINT HANDLING
---------------------
Complaints are treated as permanent:
  - Every SES complaint is persisted in `SesEvent` with the complaintFeedbackType.
  - The recipient is immediately added to `SuppressedEmail` with reason = COMPLAINT — no further mail of any kind will be sent to that address from our system.
  - The account-level SES suppression list is also enabled for COMPLAINT reasons.
We treat complaint rate as an operational alarm and will pause sending if we approach AWS's recommended thresholds.

5. UNSUBSCRIBE
--------------
Because every email we send is transactional (verification, password reset, welcome) — i.e. directly required to complete a user action the recipient just initiated — there is no recurring stream to unsubscribe from. Each recipient receives at most three lifetime emails from us (one of each type). Users can also delete their account at any time from the dashboard (https://dash.putforshare.com), which removes them from any further delivery. The `hi@putforshare.com` Reply-To address is monitored and any reply asking us to stop is honoured immediately by adding the address to `SuppressedEmail`.
