USE CASE DESCRIPTION (paste into "Use case description" field):
------------------------------------------------------------

We are requesting production access to send transactional email to users who sign up on PutForShare (https://putforshare.com), an online marketplace in India for buying and selling pre-loved books.

The reason we need to leave the SES sandbox is that the recipients of our emails are NEW USER SIGNUPS - their email addresses cannot be pre-verified with SES. In sandbox mode, every account verification email, password reset, and welcome message fails because the recipient is not a verified SES identity. Without production access, we cannot complete the user signup flow.

Scope of email - transactional only, three templates currently in production:
  1. Email address verification (sent immediately on signup; required to complete account creation).
  2. Password reset (sent only on the user's explicit 'forgot password' request).
  3. Welcome onboarding (sent once after the email address is verified).

We do not send promotional or marketing email. We do not import third-party mailing lists. Every recipient has explicitly created an account on our platform using their own email address - account creation constitutes opt-in.

Bounce and complaint handling (already implemented in the application):
Our Django backend (apps.notifications) processes SES feedback notifications via Anymail's tracking signal (apps/notifications/signals.py):
  - Every SES event (Send, Delivery, Bounce, Complaint, Reject) is persisted in a SesEvent audit log.
  - On permanent bounce or complaint, the recipient address is immediately added to a SuppressedEmail table with the reason recorded.
  - The SuppressedEmail table is checked before every outgoing send; suppressed recipients are skipped at the application layer, before SES is called.
  - Soft bounces use Celery exponential-backoff retries (max 5 attempts).
  - The account-level SES suppression list is enabled for both BOUNCE and COMPLAINT reasons.

Domain and authentication (ap-south-1):
  - putforshare.com is verified in this region.
  - DKIM signing is enabled (RSA 2048-bit, status SUCCESS).
  - A dedicated configuration set (pfs-configuration-set) is linked.
  - From: notifications@putforshare.com.
  - Reply-To: hi@putforshare.com (monitored shared inbox for replies and abuse reports).

Expected volume:
  - First 1-3 months: ~100-300 emails/day (matching early signup rate).
  - 6-12 months: scaling to 2,000-5,000 emails/day as the user base grows.
  - No bulk or burst sending - emails are dispatched per-user-action.

We will continue to monitor SES bounce and complaint rates against AWS recommended thresholds and will adjust our verification flow if rates approach the warning levels.
